Research Interests

    My research focuses on analyzing the behavior of language models in natural language processing and graph-based learning, Specifically:
  • Steering LLM's behavior: Through system messages, users can assign specific roles, perform intended tasks, incorporate background information, specify various output formats and communication styles. We provide data construction pipeline SysGen to achieve system roles.
  • Chronological Knowledge: In the era of LLMs, we find ourselves in a constantly evolving and advancing landscape. Amid this progress, my aim is to explore how LLMs comprehend and respond to chronological knowledge. In this process, we introduce ChroKnowledge, a non-parametric framework designed to elicit chronological knowledge. Furthermore, by analyzing the internal head attention, we identify a temporal head that governs the generation process in relation to temporal knowledge.
  • Question Answering: I study how to develop methods for specific domain such as biomedical and clinical. I study for the method of real-time question answering (CovidASK), solving specific challenging problem (BioBERT-QA, Transferability QA, and Data Centric QA). These days, I study to address patients' questions which could truly help in real world situations: Self-BioRAG and OLAPH.
  • Named Entity Recognition: My primary goal for NER task is to extract necessary biomedical knowledge from the ever-growing biomedical literature for domain researchers. Thus, I made automatic system to extract biomedical knowledge from literature (BERN, BERN2), consistent prediction in document context (ConNER).
  • Graph-based Learning: My earlier works include systems for graph neural networks which are widely used in representation learning on graphs including GTN, FastGTN, HATS, and PubMed KG.
