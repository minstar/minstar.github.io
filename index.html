Minbyul Jeong

Hi! I am Minbyul Jeong, an AI Research Engineer on the Large Language Model team at Upstage, where I work on the Solar model series. I received my Ph.D. at Korea University under the supervision of Professor Jaewoo Kang. I'm always passionate about solving real-world problems. My primary goal is to enable Aritifical Intelligence to help people around the world lead better lives. Ultimately, I aim to develop personalized healthcare AI systems that can diagnose and cure individual diseases, empowering everyone to manage their own health effectively. Toward this goal, I currently build search and tool-using AI agents that retrieve evidence, reason over multiple turns, and act reliably on real-world tasks.

Research Interests

    My research focuses on analyzing the behavior of language models in natural language processing and graph-based learning, Specifically:
  • Large Language Models (Industry Experience): Since joining Upstage, I have been actively involved in developing the Solar model series. My contributions include the research and development of Solar-Pro-Preview, Solar-Pro, Solar-Pro2, and Solar-Open, focusing on enhancing model performance, efficiency, and real-world applicability through innovative scaling and fine-tuning strategies.
  • Search & Agents: I build benchmarks and training environments for tool-using language agents — measuring and improving how they search, retrieve evidence, and reason over multiple turns. This includes an agentic biomedical research benchmark with retrieval-grounded citation faithfulness (OpenBioRQ), a reinforcement-learning gym for multi-turn medical agents (Healthcare AI GYM), and a Korean breadth-search benchmark for web agents (Ko-WideSearch).
  • Steering LLM's behavior: Through system messages, users can assign specific roles, perform intended tasks, incorporate background information, specify various output formats and communication styles. We provide data construction pipeline SysGen to achieve system roles.
  • Chronological Knowledge: In the era of LLMs, we find ourselves in a constantly evolving and advancing landscape. Amid this progress, my aim is to explore how LLMs comprehend and respond to chronological knowledge. In this process, we introduce ChroKnowledge, a non-parametric framework designed to elicit chronological knowledge. Furthermore, by analyzing the internal head attention, we identify a temporal head that governs the generation process in relation to temporal knowledge.
  • Question Answering: I study how to develop methods for specific domain such as biomedical and clinical. I study for the method of real-time question answering (CovidASK), solving specific challenging problem (BioBERT-QA, Transferability QA, and Data Centric QA). These days, I study to address patients' questions which could truly help in real world situations: Self-BioRAG and OLAPH.
  • Named Entity Recognition: My primary goal for NER task is to extract necessary biomedical knowledge from the ever-growing biomedical literature for domain researchers. Thus, I made automatic system to extract biomedical knowledge from literature (BERN, BERN2), consistent prediction in document context (ConNER).
  • Graph-based Learning: My earlier works include systems for graph neural networks which are widely used in representation learning on graphs including GTN, FastGTN, HATS, and PubMed KG.

OpenBioRQ: Unsolved Biomedical Research Questions for Agents
Minbyul Jeong,
Preprint.

arXiv / code / project page / dataset

We present OpenBioRQ, an agentic benchmark of 12,553 genuinely unsolved biomedical research questions across 12 domains. Without fixed answer keys, it scores agents on retrieval-grounded tool use and citation faithfulness, exposing wrong-paper citations (15.9%) and "agentic collapse" that closed-form medical QA cannot reveal.

Ko-WideSearch: A Korean Breadth-Search Benchmark for Web Agents
Minbyul Jeong,
Preprint.

project page / code / dataset

Web-agent benchmarks mostly measure depth — pinning one obscure answer behind a chain of constraints. Ko-WideSearch measures breadth: enumerate every member of a closed set and fill each item's attributes. Agents recover set membership well (92.8% Item-F1) but fail to complete rows (53.7% Row-F1).

Healthcare AI GYM for Medical Agents
Minbyul Jeong,
Preprint.

arXiv / code / project page

We present a gymnasium-compatible environment with 10 clinical domains and 3.6K+ tasks for training medical agents via multi-turn reinforcement learning. We introduce Turn-level Truncated On-Policy Distillation (TT-OPD) to stabilize training and improve multi-turn clinical reasoning.
